Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple



Tirumala Venkateswara Temple

The ancient and revered Sri Venkateswara Temple is situated on the seventh peak of Venkatachala in the Seshachalam range, next to the southern bank of Sri Swami Pushkarini. The temple features a remarkable seven-foot idol of Lord Venkateswara, drawing millions of devotees and tourists each year. Believed to have origins dating back to 1200 BC, the expansive shrine covers several acres and measures 415 feet in length and 263 feet in width. It includes three prakaras or enclosures surrounding the main sanctum of the deity. The temple has a metal sealing that is approximately 1,000 years old and contains various chambers for storing ornaments, holy silks, fresh garlands, and a sandalwood grinder. Additionally, it features a Potu (kitchen) for preparing Laddu prasadam for visitors, as well as a dedicated kitchen where daily offerings (naivedyam) for the Lord are prepared continuously throughout the day.

Sri Swayambhu Varasiddhi Vinayaka Swamy



Sri Swayambhu Varasiddhi Vinayaka Swamy


in Kanipakm, Chittoor Dt, Andhra Pradesh. Background Story of Kanipakam Temple: Temple is located in a small village called Kanipakam. In Tamil "Kani" means wetland and "Pakam" means flow of water into wetland.According to the legend of the temple there were three brothers and each one had a handicap. Viz., Dumb, Deaf and Blind. They were earning out their livelihood by cultivating a small piece of land. In olden days water was drawn from the well by way of 'Piccota System'. As one of them used to irrigate the field through the channels, the other two used to ply on the Piccota. On one such operation they found that the water in the well got dried up and they could no longer continue their job. One of them got into the well and started digging it up. He was taken aback to see the iron implement hitting a stone like formation. Later, he was shocked to see blood oozing out from it. Within a few seconds the entire water in the well, turned blood red in colour. Thus, startled by this divine sight, all the three became normal getting rid of their deformities. As soon as the villagers came to know about this miracle, they thronged to the well and tried to deepen the well further. But their attempt proved futile because the 'swayambhu' idol (the self-manifested) of Lord Vinayaka emerged from the swirling waters

SRI PRASANNA VENKATESWARA SWAMY TEMPLE, APPALAYAGUNTA


Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy Temple is located about 16 km from Tirupati. This temple has its uniqueness with the presence of the presiding deity, Lord Sri Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy in the 'Abhaya Hasta posture', which is considered to be the final posture of blessing. The blissful attitude of the God is pleasing and rewarding to a devotee who wants to delve into the realms of Swami.Importance of Prasanna Venkateswara SwamyTemple This is the place where Lord Venkateswara Swamy honored the conglomerate of Sri Siddeshwara and other sages on the occasion of his marriage. Since the temple was the place of union of the Lord with his consort Sri Padmavathi Ammavaru, it is considered to be the most auspicious and bountiful here. Pilgrims believe that a sight of this Lord fulfilling their wishes as the Lord is in the Abhaya Hasta position. Another important feature of this ancient temple is the presence of God Vayu - Lord of Wind who provides relief to devotees suffering from chronic diseases.The temple was built by the King of Karvetinagaram, Sri Venkata Perumalaraju Brahmadeva Maharaj in 1232 AD and includes the shrine of Lord Anjaneya Swamy located in front of the temple. The temple also houses idols of Goddess Padmavathi and Smt Andal Devi in ​​the temple.This temple was taken over by TTD in 1988 and all the rituals follow the principles of Vaikhanasa Agama.

Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Sanctuary, moreover known as Alamelumangapuram, is found within the town of Tiruchanur, close the city of Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh. It is devoted to Goddess Padmavathi, who is accepted to be the partner of Ruler Venkateswara, the directing god of the celebrated Tirupati Sanctuary. The Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple is one of the foremost critical and venerated sanctuaries in South India and pulls in thousands of aficionados and sightseers each year.

History of Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple


The history of Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Sanctuary dates back to the 8th century when the sanctuary was at first built by the Pallavas. The sanctuary was afterward redesigned and extended amid the rule of the Vijayanagara Domain within the 14th century. The temple's design could be a mix of Pallava, Chola and Vijayanagara styles. Concurring to legend, Goddess Padmavathi was born as the girl of the Lord of the seven slopes, Akasha Raja. The goddess was said to have risen from a lotus bloom in a lake within the adjacent town of Padmavathi Kshetram. She was found by Master Venkateswara whereas he was on a chasing undertaking, and they fell in adore. They got hitched in a fantastic ceremony, which is believed to have taken put at this sanctuary. Their wedding is celebrated each year within the sanctuary amid the Navaratri celebration.
